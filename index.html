I am a demographer and sociologist with expertise in population health and aging. My research falls into five areas: (1) Excess mortality during the Covid-19 pandemic, (2) Determinants of mortality and life expectancy trends, (3) Chronic disease, pain, and disability across the life course, (4) Substance use, tobacco, and e-cigarettes, (5) Access to care for chronic diseases in low- and middle-income countries.


Through my research and dissemination efforts, I strive to reveal the social and structural factors that influence health across the life course, inform public health policies that center health equity, and contribute to evidence-based reforms of public health administrative systems such as the death investigation system.


I received my B.A. in Environmental Studies from Bates College, my M.A. in Demography from the University of Pennsylvania, and my PhD in Demography and Sociology from the University of Pennsylvania. Prior to my doctoral studies, I was a post-bachelor fellow at the Harvard Initiative for Global Health in Cambridge, MA.


I currently work as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Global Health at Boston University School of Public Health.
